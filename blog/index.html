Introducing Juris-M: the successor to MLZ

Followers of MLZ may have noticed recent mentions of a rebranding of the tool as “Juris-M.” This is its home. Welcome: I hope you enjoy the new digs. This note explains the reason for the rebranding, introduces some of its newly added features, and peeks forward to some important developments on the horizon.

The two items at the top of the agenda are these:

Juris-M is new product

Firefox is on the verge of requiring that all Add-ons be signed by the author or institution that issues them. MLZ began as a (relatively) casual fork of Zotero, and it used the same product ID as the official product. That was a choice born of inexperience on my part, but it will soon be flat-out impermissible.

Juris-M has a unique ID all its own, and can be distributed under the new policy. However, it is incompatible as software with Zotero (that is, it would be impossibly confusing to have two full reference manager interfaces running in a single browser). To play nice with Zotero (and with a previously installed MLZ), Juris-M will disable itself on installation if it detects an enabled Add-on with the Zotero ID. If you receive a warning message when installing Juris-M, do not be alarmed: just disable the old Add-on and re-enable Juris-M, and all will be well.
