Briefly describe your project

"Juris-M" is a massively awesome reference manager that can do legal referencing and works with multiple languages.

Mention something interactive

Click on "Style Manager" and type something where it says "Enter a country or institution." Select a country, click "Load," and when it's ready click on "Sampler" and drag things around.

List up things to help users get started

To use Juris-M, install the Desktop Client (a Firefox plugin), and the Abbreviation Filter. (The desktop client looks a lot like Zotero, because that is what it is based on.)

Talk a little about history and stuff

Juris-M is a rebranding of two projects that I have been maintaining for several years. Multilingual Zotero (aka MLZ) will be released in due course as "the Juris-M client"; and the CSL-m extensions to the Citation Style Language will be known as "the Juris-M citation language."The new names are only that; nothing will change in the relation between the work here and the mainstream projects on which it heavily depends. The main effect will be to save some typing, and to avoid some confusion: the tools that are now Juris-M have enjoyed an amicable and productive arm's-length relationship with Zotero and CSL to the present, and that will continue.

— Frank Bennett, 10 March 2015
