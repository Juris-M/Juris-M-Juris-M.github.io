Juris-M is a reference manager designed for the 21st century, with robust support for multilingual citations, and next-generation legal referencing capabilities. Juris-M development is driven by a desire to broaden and deepen public discourse by lowering the obstacles to participation. In this case, the ideal is a friend of the pragmatic: if you work across multiple languages, Juris-M was built with your needs in mind. There is no charge for taking a closer look—and you may be glad that you did.

Components

The system is made up of several components: a Desktop Client (Firefox plugin) derived from the Zotero research platform; an Abbreviation Filter (Firefox plugin) for style-specific abbreviation support; and the Juris-M extended version of the Citation Style Language.

Legal Styles

Juris-M enables modular application of legal styles, to permit precise, jurisdiction-specific generation of references. To create or modify a jurisdiction module, use the Style Manager provided by this site.

Rebranding

Juris-M is a rebranding of two projects that I have been maintaining for several years. From the formal rollout of this website, Multilingual Zotero (aka MLZ) will be released as "the Juris-M client"; and the CSL-m extensions to the Citation Style Language will be known as "the Juris-M citation language." The new names are only that; nothing will change in the relation between the work here and these two mainstream projects on which it heavily depends. The main effect will be to save some typing, and to avoid some confusion: the tools that are now Juris-M have enjoyed an amicable and productive arm's-length relationship with Zotero and CSL to the present, and that will continue.
